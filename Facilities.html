Facilities Provided by Agricultural Schemes

1. Subsidized Fertilizers

  • Farmers receive fertilizers at a subsidized price.
  • Government provides subsidies to fertilizer manufacturers and distributors.
  • Ensures timely availability of fertilizers during the cropping season.

2. Financial Aid and Subsidies

  • Kisan Credit Card (KCC): A loan facility for farmers to get easy access to credit.
  • Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY): Crop insurance scheme for risk mitigation.
  • Interest Subvention Scheme: Interest subsidies for loans taken by farmers.
  • Credit guarantee schemes for agriculture-based activities.

3. Insurance Schemes

  • Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY): Offers crop insurance to protect farmers against natural disasters, pest attacks, or any damage.
  • National Crop Insurance Programme (NCIP): Protects farmers from risks related to agriculture.

4. Market Support and Price Assurance

  • Minimum Support Price (MSP): Guarantee for farmers to sell crops at a minimum price.
  • National Agricultural Market (eNAM): A platform for farmers to sell produce online, getting better market prices.
  • Price Support Scheme: Government buys crops from farmers when market prices fall below MSP.

5. Subsidized Equipment and Technology Support

  • Subsidy on agricultural equipment like tractors, pumps, etc.
  • Promotion of advanced agricultural technologies like drip irrigation, which increases yield with reduced water usage.
  • National Mission on Agricultural Extension and Technology (NMAET): Supports farmers in adopting modern technology.

6. Training and Extension Services

  • Government provides training on sustainable farming, modern techniques, and efficient resource utilization.
  • Krishi Vigyan Kendras (KVKs): Rural extension services providing training and technical advice.

7. Horticulture and Organic Farming Support

  • Subsidies for horticulture farming, including fruit and vegetable cultivation.
  • Support for organic farming practices and certification.
  • National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA): Aims to promote sustainable practices in farming.

8. Irrigation and Water Conservation

  • Subsidies for setting up irrigation systems like drip irrigation and sprinklers.
  • Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY): Focused on increasing irrigation coverage.
  • Water harvesting and conservation techniques supported through schemes.

9. Livestock and Animal Husbandry Support

  • Subsidies on livestock feed, vaccines, and healthcare.
  • National Livestock Mission: Promotes sustainable livestock production and health.
  • Support for dairy farming, poultry, and fisheries.

10. Agri-Processing and Infrastructure Development

  • Financial assistance for setting up food processing units and cold storage.
  • Infrastructure development for rural markets.
  • Pradhan Mantri Kisan Sampada Yojana (PMKSY): Supports the establishment of agri-processing and value addition infrastructure.

  • PM-Kisan: Direct income support to farmers.
  • Soil Health Card Scheme: Helps farmers understand soil quality and improve crop yield.

12. Farm Mechanization and Rural Connectivity

  • Subsidies for purchasing modern farm equipment.
  • Government support for improving rural roads and connectivity to enhance supply chain efficiency.
